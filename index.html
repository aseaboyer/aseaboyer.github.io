Drupal Upgrade Epic

As Drupal 7 neared end of life, DigiPen's Campus websites needed to be upgraded. Our Drupal 7 configurations relied on several modules which were no longer seeing active development. I made the decision that it would be best to begin development on a new theme. This allowed us to implement newer Drupal methodologies.

We created a new server with Drupal 9 and created JSON feeds on our development servers which could be consumed by the Drupal 9 instances. This allowed me to continue developing the new site without interrupting our content team who continued their updates on the Drupal 7 sites. I was able to periodically import content updates as I worked using the Feeds Module.

After our first site was released, Drupal 10 became available. We upgraded that server and then cloned it to quick-start the process for our second campus site, and again for our final site.
